Wikipedia will be searched for supporting info; only angles whose queries are likely to land will be used.

Rules:
- Building blocks should be true; the conclusion is bogus. Queries must not presuppose the claim.
- Each search query is factoid-specific: we are searching for citations to things we already know. No broad exploratory fishing.
- Prefer known-to-be-true facts (e.g. "Russia military dolphins") over speculative correlations (e.g. "squirrel populations near Russian embassies").
- Avoid weak or superficial analogies (e.g. "squirrels move fast like spies")—the argument must be compelling and the queries must find each piece of evidence separately.
- Keep arguments short and simple. Aim for 5–10.
- Keep the number of search queries minimal. Ideally 1-2
